Brand Book & Design System

A family venture studio cultivating ideas worth growing.

Seattle, WA  ·  satsumaventures.com  ·  2026

01

Brand Foundation

Name

Satsuma Ventures

A variety of mandarin orange — easy to peel, naturally sweet, grown in groves. The name grounds the studio in something tangible and seasonal: the idea that good things take time to cultivate, and that the fruit is worth finding.

Tagline

A family venture studio cultivating ideas worth growing.

Mission

Satsuma Ventures is a private family holding studio based in Seattle. It serves as the parent entity for a portfolio of internally ideated and built businesses — concepts developed, validated, and spun out as independent entities over time. The studio may also support select angel investments.

Positioning

Satsuma sits at the intersection of founder thinking and patient capital. The studio cultivates a small number of ideas with genuine care, on a long horizon — building the structure that lets them bear fruit.

Tone of voice

Warm but substantive. Lightly narrative — a studio with a point of view, not a corporate holding vehicle. Confident without being boastful. Speaks like a thoughtful founder, not a fund manager.

  • Write as a person, not an institution
  • Use plain language — no jargon, no buzzwords
  • Be specific rather than vague about what the studio does
  • Never sound like a VC pitch deck
  • Unhurried — the studio thinks in years, not quarters

Usage principles
  • Grove is the dominant structural color — navigation, headings, buttons, links
  • Satsuma is used sparingly but visibly — section numbers, eyebrows, en-dash bullets, the fruit in the mark
  • Orange should never fill a large region — it earns its place through small, repeated moments
  • Linen is the default page background — warm and paper-like, never plain white
  • Warm Bark is for cards and inline code chips — adds warmth at smaller surfaces
  • Mist remains available as a cool, lighter surface alternative — used rarely, for contrast
  • Sage is decorative — it appears in its own showcase and rare moments, not as a default label color

Spacing & Layout

White space is the primary design element. Spacing follows an 8px base, with named tokens that map predictably across surfaces. Generous margins and line heights at all times — the brand thinks in years, not quarters, and the page should feel that way.

Layout rules
  • Page content caps at 860px; reading copy caps at 680px or 60ch, whichever is tighter
  • Left-aligned text as default — centered only for intentional hero moments
  • Open, unhurried page structures — not information-dense

06

Elevation & Motion

Satsuma has no drop-shadow system. Elevation is communicated through color (Warm Bark surfaces against Mist) and hairline borders. Drop shadows, glows, and glassmorphism all read as corporate and are excluded from the brand.

Surface tiers

Warm Bark

Card surface against the Mist page background. Hairline border optional. No shadow.

Deep Ink

High-contrast surface. Used for logo cards on dark, occasional inverted moments.

Motion

Quiet. Almost imperceptible. The studio thinks in years; motion should reinforce that pace.

Easingcubic-bezier(0.22, 0.61, 0.36, 1)Soft ease-out
Duration · fast160msColor transitions, hover
Duration · base220msButtons, links, focus rings
Duration · slow400msRare, considered moments only
  • Permitted: color transitions on links and buttons (fade to Satsuma or darker Grove)
  • Forbidden: bounces, springs, scale-on-hover, parallax, page-load reveal sequences
  • Focus rings: 2px Sage outline with 2px offset — no glow, no animation
  • No opacity-based hover states — color shifts only

08

Visual Principles

Imagery
  • Prefer typography-led layouts over photography
  • When photography is used: natural light, organic textures, never staged or stock-feeling
  • Botanical and natural references are welcome — they reinforce the brand metaphor
  • Avoid heavy full-bleed photography, dark hero sections, or high-contrast graphic treatments
  • No protection gradients over photos — composition handles legibility instead
Iconography

Satsuma's source brand uses no iconography. Words and white space carry the meaning. If an icon is genuinely required, use Lucide at 1.5px stroke, 20–24px, Grove color. Never Material Icons, Font Awesome, emoji, or decorative Unicode beyond · — – ★.

References

The visual neighborhood Satsuma belongs in: matterfamilyoffice.com, whiteoakoffice.com. Open feel, generous white space, not rigid or corporate. Typography-led, calm, and considered.

What to avoid
  • Anything that reads as a VC fund or institutional investor
  • Aggressive CTAs or conversion-optimized language
  • Orange used prominently — it is an accent, not a theme color
  • Rigid grids, corporate templates, or stiff layouts
  • Gradients, drop shadows, glassmorphism, neumorphism
  • Emoji, sticker-style decoration, hand-drawn doodles
